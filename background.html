Background
of the conference.

Water is the life-line of our green planet.It is vital for all lives on earth and human existance.All kind of economic activities,food production as well as ecology depend on availability of safe water. In recent years fresh water has become scare in many parts of the world .People are facing more frequent and more intense natural disaster as heavy rainfall,flood,draught.Environment has become more vulnerable due to climate change.Draught afflicts some of the world poorest countries,worsening poverty,hunger and malnutrition.Population growth,fast urbanization,diversified economic activities, technological change and changing water consumtion patterns are recognized as the the main factors influncing diverse uses of water.Climate change brings in an additional dimension inwater domain. The high demand for water satisfy the needs of increasing population is putting tremendous stress on the environment.Moreover there is lacking efficient and effective management of water resource at different parts of the world in terms of availability,adequacy,quality and equality.Implementation of environmental safeguards and measures to improve water effieciency and to minimize pollution are failing short in most sectors. Many regions particullarly poorer communities has become vulnerable due to water realated disasters that can adversly affect livelihood,assets and economic activities.Access to basic water services for safe drinking water,sanitation and hygenic remain insufficient across the developing countries. There are many water related issues which demand greater attention such as:

  • MDG goal for drinking water is not achieved yet.663 million people in the world do not have acecess to improve drinking water.About 1.8 billion people globally use sources of drinking water that have microbial contamination
  • About 2.4 billion people do not have access to basic sanitation.
  • Each day nearly 1000 children die due to preventable water born diseases.
  • Water scarcity affects livelihood of more than 40% of the global population and the percentage is geeting higher.
  • Even week many(number??) people die of diseases caused by inferior quality of water and unsanitary conditions,among them 90 percent are children up to 5 years of age.
  • Drought and desertification threaten the access to means of subsistance for over 1.2 billion people in the world.
  • More than 80% of untreated wastewater resulting from human activities is discharged into environment.
